Module 01 of 05

Phishing & Social Engineering

Phishing is the most common way attackers target AKG. They pretend to be a trusted person or service to steal your credentials or trick you into clicking a malicious link.

How to spot a phishing email

Suspicious sender domain
Urgent or threatening language
Unexpected password reset
Link URL doesn't match text
Request to bypass normal process
Changed invoice bank details
Unexpected attachment

Real example

Phishing
  • From: it-support@akg-helpdesk.net
  • "Your VPN expired. Click here to reactivate in 24 hours."
Legitimate
  • From: helpdesk@akg.com
  • "Update your VPN via Software Center → search GlobalProtect."

Other attack types

Phone (Vishing)

"This is IT. We need your password to fix your account."

Executive fraud (BEC)

Fake CEO email requesting urgent wire transfer.

Tailgating

Unknown person asks you to hold a secure door.

What to do

1
Don't click anything

Not links, not attachments, not even "unsubscribe".

2
Click "Report Phishing" in Outlook

Notifies IT Security and removes the email from your inbox.

3
If you already clicked — call IT now

Ext. 2200 or it-security@akg.com. Early reporting limits the damage.

Golden rule

IT will never ask for your password — by phone, email, or chat. Always refuse and report.

Module 02 of 05

Password & MFA Policy

Stolen credentials are behind most unauthorized access incidents. AKG enforces strict password and MFA requirements on all systems.

Password rules

Required
  • Minimum 14 characters
  • Uppercase + lowercase + digit + symbol
  • Changed every 90 days
  • Use an approved password manager
Prohibited
  • Using your name or "AKG" in password
  • Reusing your last 12 passwords
  • Sharing your password with anyone
  • Writing passwords on sticky notes
Weak — don't use

AKG2024! — Dictionary word + year. Cracked instantly.

Strong — use this style

Tr!#9vKm@wqLz4 — Random, 14+ chars. Store in password manager.

Password manager

Use KeePass (offline) or Bitwarden (cloud-synced). Both are corporate-approved. Contact IT Helpdesk if you need setup help.

MFA — mandatory on all systems

1
Enrol in Microsoft Authenticator on Day 1

Required for VPN, Microsoft 365, Azure, and all internal apps.

2
Use app-based TOTP — not SMS

SMS OTP is no longer accepted on critical AKG systems.

3
Never approve a prompt you didn't initiate

Verify the number on-screen matches the Authenticator code before approving.

MFA Fatigue Attack

Attackers send repeated push notifications hoping you'll tap "Approve" to stop them. If you get unexpected prompts — deny all, change your password, call IT immediately.

Module 03 of 05

Acceptable Use Policy

The AKG Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines how you may use company devices, networks, and data. Violations can lead to disciplinary action — or legal liability.

Allowed vs. not allowed

Permitted
  • Business use of corporate devices
  • Brief personal browsing during breaks
  • Software from the Software Center only
  • VPN when working remotely
Prohibited
  • Installing unauthorized software
  • Personal USB drives on corporate network
  • Storing work data on personal cloud (Google Drive, Dropbox)
  • Disabling antivirus or security tools
  • Using personal email for work documents
Zero tolerance

Bypassing or disabling security controls is a zero-tolerance violation — even if your intent was productivity.

Data classification

Public

No restrictions. Approved marketing content only.

Internal

AKG employees only. Do not share externally without permission.

Confidential

Need-to-know. Contracts, financials, HR records.

Restricted

PII, customer data, security configs. Written approval required.

When in doubt about a document's classification — treat it as Internal and ask before sharing.

Module 04 of 05

Incident Reporting

The faster you report a security incident, the less damage it causes. You don't need to be certain — if something feels wrong, report it.

What to report

Clicked a phishing link
Unexpected MFA prompts
Antivirus or EDR alert
Unusual device behaviour
Lost or stolen device/badge
Someone asking for your credentials
Wrong email recipient for sensitive data
Important

Never try to fix it yourself. Doing so can destroy forensic evidence. Report first — IT Security will guide you through next steps.

How to report

1
Critical incident? Call immediately

IT Security: Ext. 2200 — for active breaches, ransomware, stolen devices.

2
Non-urgent? Email IT Security

it-security@akg.com — include what happened, when, and which systems are involved.

3
Always raise a ticket

IT Portal → Security → Report Incident — even if you already called.

4
Don't discuss it externally

No social media, no client calls — until IT Security says it's cleared.

No-blame policy

If you report in good faith, you will not face disciplinary action — even if you clicked something. Reporting late is the problem, not the click.

Module 05 of 05

Physical Security & Clean Desk

No firewall protects an unlocked screen or a confidential document left on a desk. Physical security is everyone's responsibility.

Access badge rules

Do
  • Keep your badge on you at all times
  • Report a lost badge immediately — Reception + IT Ext. 2200
  • Challenge anyone without a visible visitor badge
Don't
  • Lend your badge to anyone — including colleagues
  • Allow tailgating through secured doors
  • Leave visitors unescorted inside AKG premises

Clean desk — every time you leave your desk

1
Lock your screen: Windows + L

Auto-lock activates after 5 minutes — but don't wait for it.

2
File or lock away documents

Sensitive documents go in your locked drawer — not left on your desk.

3
Shred, don't bin

Any document with Internal, Confidential, or Restricted data must be shredded.

USB drop attacks

Real attack technique

Attackers leave labelled USB drives (e.g. "AKG Salaries 2026") in car parks hoping curiosity wins. Never plug in a found USB device. Hand it directly to IT Security.

Daily habit

Before you walk away from your desk: Win+L → file documents → visual check. Make it automatic.
