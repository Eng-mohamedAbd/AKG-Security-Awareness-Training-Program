# Module 01 — Phishing & Social Engineering

**Training Program:** AKG IT Security Awareness (IT-SEC-AWARE-01)  
**Estimated Read Time:** ~6 minutes

---

## What Is Phishing?

Phishing is a deceptive attack in which an adversary impersonates a trusted entity — a colleague, vendor, Microsoft, or AKG's own IT department — to trick you into:

- Revealing your credentials
- Clicking a malicious link or opening an infected attachment
- Transferring funds or sensitive data

At AKG, over **80% of security incidents** begin with a phishing email or social engineering attempt.

> **Important:** Spear phishing attacks are targeted. Attackers research your name, role, and manager from LinkedIn before crafting a convincing message. Personalization does not equal legitimacy.

---

## Red Flags to Identify

- Sender email domain doesn't match the organization it claims to be from
- Urgent or threatening language: *"Your account will be suspended in 2 hours"*
- Unexpected password reset, MFA prompt, or DocuSign requests you didn't initiate
- Links that don't match their display text — hover before clicking
- Requests to bypass normal process: *"Don't create a ticket, just send me the file"*
- Invoices or wire transfer requests with changed banking details
- Unsolicited attachments, even from known senders (their account may be compromised)

---

## Example: Phishing vs. Legitimate

| | Phishing | Legitimate |
|-|----------|------------|
| **Sender** | it-support@akg-helpdesk.net | helpdesk@akg.com |
| **Message** | "Your VPN license expired. Click here to reactivate within 24 hours." | "Your VPN client needs updating. Please open the Software Center." |
| **Action required** | Urgency + external link | Internal process + no link |

---

## Social Engineering Tactics

Beyond email, attackers use phone calls (vishing), SMS (smishing), and in-person pretexting:

| Tactic | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **IT impersonation** | "This is IT, we need your credentials to fix an issue on your account" |
| **Executive fraud (BEC)** | Impersonating the CEO to request urgent wire transfers |
| **Vendor pretexting** | Fake supplier calls requesting updated payment details |
| **Tailgating requests** | Asking you to hold a secure door open |

> **Rule:** IT will **never** ask for your password — by phone, email, or chat. If anyone requests your credentials, refuse and report immediately to **it-security@akg.com**.

---

## What to Do with a Suspicious Email

1. **Do not click any links or open attachments** — even "unsubscribe" links can confirm your address is active
2. **Use the "Report Phishing" button in Outlook** — this notifies the security team and removes the email from your inbox
3. **If you clicked a link — report immediately** — contact IT Security at ext. 2200 or it-security@akg.com. Early reporting limits damage significantly
