# Module 04 — Incident Reporting

**Training Program:** AKG IT Security Awareness (IT-SEC-AWARE-01)  
**Estimated Read Time:** ~5 minutes

---

## Why Reporting Matters

Early incident reporting dramatically reduces the impact of security events. Organizations that detect and report quickly contain breaches in **days**; those that delay take **months**. You are part of AKG's detection capability — not a passive bystander.

---

## What Qualifies as a Security Incident?

Report any of the following immediately:

| Event | Report? |
|-------|---------|
| Received or clicked a phishing email or suspicious link | ✅ Yes |
| Unexpected MFA prompts or account lockouts | ✅ Yes |
| Antivirus or EDR alert on your device | ✅ Yes |
| Unusual device behavior — high CPU, unknown processes | ✅ Yes |
| Lost or stolen laptop, phone, or access badge | ✅ Yes |
| Unauthorized person requesting access or credentials | ✅ Yes |
| Accidental data disclosure to the wrong recipient | ✅ Yes |
| Something "just feels wrong" with your device | ✅ Yes |

> **Critical:** Never attempt to investigate or remediate a suspected incident on your own. This can destroy forensic evidence and worsen containment. **Report — do not touch.**

---

## How to Report

### Step 1 — For Critical Incidents (active breach, ransomware, stolen device)

📞 **Call IT Security immediately: Ext. 2200**  
Do not rely on email for time-sensitive events.

### Step 2 — For Non-Urgent Incidents

📧 **Email: it-security@akg.com**  
Include: what happened, when it occurred, which systems or data are involved, and any screenshots if available.

### Step 3 — Formal Documentation

🖥️ **IT Portal → Security → Report Incident**  
Use this for formal ticket creation alongside your phone/email report.

### Step 4 — Information Containment

🔇 **Do not discuss externally**  
Do not discuss potential incidents with non-IT staff, clients, or on social media until authorized by IT Security or management.

---

## What Happens After You Report

1. IT Security acknowledges and logs the incident within 15 minutes during business hours
2. Initial triage is performed — you may be asked for additional details
3. Containment actions are taken (account isolation, device quarantine, etc.)
4. You will be kept informed of resolution status
5. A post-incident review is conducted for significant events

---

## No-Blame Policy

AKG operates a **no-blame policy** for security incidents reported in good faith.

- If you clicked a phishing link — **report it, you will not be disciplined**
- False positives are welcomed — they are far better than missed detections
- Delays in reporting, however, can result in significantly worse outcomes and may be treated differently under policy

> **When in doubt, report it.** There are no stupid incident reports.
