# Module 05 — Physical Security & Clean Desk

**Training Program:** AKG IT Security Awareness (IT-SEC-AWARE-01)  
**Estimated Read Time:** ~4 minutes

---

## Why Physical Security Matters

Digital security controls are meaningless if an unauthorized person can walk up to an unlocked workstation or pick up a confidential document from a desk. Physical security is as important as your firewall.

---

## Access Control

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Badge is personal | Never lend your badge to anyone — including colleagues |
| Lost badge | Report immediately to Reception **and** IT Security (Ext. 2200) |
| Tailgating | Do not allow anyone to follow you through secured doors |
| Visitors | Escort all visitors at all times inside AKG premises |
| Unknown visitors | Challenge any person without a visible visitor badge — politely but firmly |

> **Social Engineering Risk:** Tailgating is a common physical intrusion tactic. A well-dressed person carrying boxes and acting confident counts on you feeling rude to challenge them. Always verify — it is expected and encouraged at AKG.

---

## Clean Desk Policy

All employees must maintain a clean desk whenever they are away from their workstation — even for short periods.

### Required Actions

- **Lock your screen before leaving your desk:** `Windows key + L`
- Store sensitive documents in locked drawers when not in use
- Shred documents containing Confidential or Restricted information — never place them in the bin
- Do not write passwords on sticky notes or leave them near your workstation
- Clear your desk at end of day — no documents, removable media, or handwritten notes left exposed

---

## Workstation Security

| Control | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Auto screen lock | Triggers after 5 minutes — do not disable |
| Screen visibility | Use a privacy screen in open-plan or public areas |
| USB devices | Never connect unknown USB devices to your workstation |
| Physical locking | Secure laptop with a Kensington lock in shared spaces |
| Sensitive data on screen | Avoid visible display of sensitive data in public areas |

> **USB Drop Attack:** Leaving a labelled USB drive in a car park or lobby is a documented attack technique. Never plug in a found USB device — hand it directly to IT Security.

---

## Working in Public Spaces

When working in cafes, airports, or co-working spaces:

- VPN must be active before accessing any AKG resource
- Position your screen away from others or use a privacy screen
- Lock your screen before walking away — even for seconds
- Never conduct sensitive calls or meetings in public where you can be overheard
- Do not print or handle physical confidential documents in public

---

## End-of-Day Checklist

Before leaving your workstation at end of day:

- [ ] Screen locked (`Win + L`)
- [ ] All documents filed or locked in drawer
- [ ] Sensitive documents shredded
- [ ] Removable media (USB, smart cards) secured
- [ ] No handwritten notes or passwords visible on or near desk
- [ ] Computer locked or shut down per department policy

---

| Unauthorized person in secure area | Security desk + IT Security |
| Found USB or suspicious device | IT Security — do not plug in |
